Before You Start

Why Non-Nitrate Juice
and EC-1118?

Non-Nitrate Juice is Critical

Preservatives like potassium sorbate, sodium benzoate, or potassium metabisulfite kill or suppress yeast. If these are in your juice, it simply will not ferment. Always check the ingredients label — use only juice marked as 100% juice with no preservatives. Ascorbic acid (vitamin C) is fine and does not affect fermentation.

EC-1118 Champagne Yeast

It ferments fast, tolerates low nutrients, and is highly alcohol-tolerant. It will drive your cider bone dry to around 1.000 SG or lower, giving roughly 6 to 7 percent ABV from standard apple juice. If you want residual sweetness, you must backsweeten after fermentation is fully complete — stabilise first with Campden plus potassium sorbate, then add sugar or non-fermentable sweetener to taste.

Headspace is Not Optional

The 0.5L headspace in a 5L demijohn is not optional. EC-1118 ferments vigorously. Without headspace the krausen foam will blow straight through your airlock and onto the floor.

The Method

Step by Step — 4.5L Batch

About 30 minutes of active work on brew day, then 3–6 weeks of patient waiting. Sanitize everything that touches the cider from step one onwards.

1. Sanitize Everything

Mix sanitizer in a clean bucket per label instructions. Fully sanitize the demijohn, bung, airlock, funnel, siphon, tubing, trial jar, and hydrometer. Do not rinse if using no-rinse sanitizer — the foam is harmless.

Stick the thermometer strip to the demijohn. Position it in its fermentation spot now — somewhere at 18–22°C, out of direct sunlight, where you won't need to move it for two weeks.

2. Prepare the Juice

Funnel 4.5L of juice into the sanitized demijohn. Leave a minimum of 0.5L headspace — this is not negotiable.

  • For unpasteurized juice: crush ½ Campden tablet, add, swirl gently, fit airlock, and wait 24 hours before proceeding
  • For pectic enzyme: add ½ tsp now, swirl to mix, and wait 12–24 hours before pitching yeast
  • Confirm temperature is 18–25°C before adding yeast

3. Record Original Gravity + Add Nutrient

Sanitize your trial jar and hydrometer. Draw a sample from the demijohn into the trial jar, float the hydrometer, and record the OG. Expect 1.045–1.055 for plain supermarket juice. Return the sample or drink it — your choice.

Add ½ tsp of yeast nutrient to the demijohn. Swirl gently to dissolve.

4. Rehydrate and Pitch Yeast

In a sanitized cup, measure 50ml of clean water at 35°C. Sprinkle EC-1118 onto the surface and wait 15 minutes without stirring, then stir gently. It should look creamy and active.

Pour the yeast slurry into the demijohn. Fit the bung and airlock — fill the airlock to the mark with sanitizer or vodka. For the first 48 hours, a blow-off tube into a jar of sanitizer is a sensible precaution if you expect vigorous foam.

5. Primary Fermentation — 7 to 14 Days

Within 12–24 hours you should see bubbles every few seconds in the airlock. Maintain 18–22°C. Do not open the demijohn.

After about 7 days, bubbling will noticeably slow. When you reach roughly 1 bubble per minute or less, wait a further 3 days and then check the final gravity.

6. Confirm Fermentation is Complete

Sanitize the trial jar and hydrometer. Pull a sample with a sanitized siphon or wine thief. Check the SG on two consecutive days. If it is stable and at or below 1.003, your cider is done.

For EC-1118, expect a FG of 0.998–1.000. Calculate ABV using: 131.25 × (OG − FG). Example: 1.050 − 1.000 = 6.56% ABV.

—

Optional: rack into a clean sanitized demijohn and allow to clear for 1–2 weeks. EC-1118 compacts well and this step is often unnecessary.

7. Bottle for Carbonation

Sanitize bottles, caps, siphon, tubing, and a small saucepan. Boil 50ml of water and dissolve 18–22g of table sugar or dextrose. Cover and allow to cool fully to room temperature.

Gently rack the cider onto the priming solution in a sanitized bottling bucket or clean demijohn. Avoid splashing — oxygen at this stage is the enemy. Stir very gently to incorporate sugar without aerating.

Siphon into bottles, leaving 2–3cm headspace. Cap immediately and label with the date.

8. Condition and Enjoy

Store bottles at 18–22°C for 2–3 weeks to develop carbonation. To test, chill one bottle for 24 hours then open carefully. If it's flat, leave the remaining bottles for another week.

Once carbonated to your liking, move to cool storage or the fridge. Cider improves noticeably over 1–3 months. EC-1118 always ferments dry — expect tart, crisp, and refreshing rather than sweet.

Safety Notes
  • Never bottle before fermentation is completely done. Confirm FG is stable below 1.003. Bottle bombs cause serious injury
  • Do not exceed 22g priming sugar for 4.5L — that is approximately 2.6 volumes CO2. More risks dangerous over-carbonation
  • All bottles must be rated for pressure. Beer bottles and champagne bottles only — no wine bottles, no mason jars
  • Sanitize everything that touches cider after brew day. One dirty implement can destroy an entire batch
  • EC-1118 is aggressive. It will blow through airlocks if you overfill. Always preserve adequate headspace
  • Sulfur or egg smell usually means yeast stress. Yeast nutrient prevents it. It often dissipates with age and aeration at serving
Troubleshooting
  • No bubbles after 24h: Check the bung seal and temperature. If still nothing, check your juice label for preservatives — then repitch fresh yeast
  • Cloudy cider: Time resolves most haze. Cold crash at 2–4°C for 3–5 days, or use pectic enzyme before pitching next time
  • Too dry or sour: EC-1118 always finishes dry. To backsweeten, stabilize with Campden plus potassium sorbate, then add sugar to taste
  • Flat bottles: Too cold, too little priming sugar, or spent yeast. Warm to 21°C for another week and invert gently to rouse the yeast
  • Gushers or over-carbed: Fermentation was incomplete when bottled, or too much sugar. Chill all bottles immediately, open with care, refrigerate and consume quickly
  • Vinegar smell: Acetobacter infection — typically from poor sanitation or oxygen exposure. Improve technique next batch. This batch is usually lost
